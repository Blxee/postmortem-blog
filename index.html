OnlineChess Outage Postmortem

All programmers will experience an outage evetually in their lives,
My first experience of an outage happened to my OnlineChess web-app, and in this blog serves as postmortem to learn from that experience.

Duration of the outage: approximately one hour.

Magnitude as a result: the whole app was down, because of SPOF.

Cause of the issue: code base crash.

Now that formalities are out of the way, let's dive to the meat of it, shall we?

Timeline:

  • 9:30 - an email reached me from the Watchdog alert, informing me of the incident.
  • 9:35 - i started investigating the logs in hopes of identifying the erro.
  • 9:45 - i had come to the conclusion that cause might be a bug from the latest deployment.
  • 10:30 - after more investigation, the root cause was identified (the bug was trying to read attributes of a possibly null value)
  • 11:05 - with quick fix everything was operating as expected again.

Identifying the error took longer than expected because it was a problem with the logic itself, after i found out exactly where the problamatic code resides, the fix was as easy as adding an if guard, that's all it took fortunately.
I admit that not testing the code thouroughly before deployment, and after this incident i stopped taking unit tests for granted.
